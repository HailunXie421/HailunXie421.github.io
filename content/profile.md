# About me

Hi, I'm Hailun. I'm actively applying for PhD programs, with research interests in the intersection of large language models and human language processing.

I received my undergraduate degree from the School of Information and Communication Engineering at Communication University of China, and later completed my graduate studies in the School of Psychology at Beijing Normal University. After graduating, I worked as a research assistant at the Shenzhen Institute of Neuroscience and as a researcher at Infinite Brain Technology in Beijing. I also contributed to the training of Doubao (ByteDance) as a freelance LLM alignment data contributor.

Language has always been a deep interest of mine, or rather, language itself is my passion. I have translated eight books, spanning popular science and science fiction. I'm fascinated by the joy of language learning and the challenge of conveying subtle meaning across languages, and it is precisely this fascination that has driven me to explore the nature of language itself. I hope to have the opportunity to use tools such as large language models and neuroimaging to make discoveries in this field I love.
